Overview
🔬
Built for Research Lab KravitzLab at Washington University in St. Louis

This project was developed for KravitzLab, a neuroscience research group studying neural circuit function and behavioral outcomes. The platform was built to support their analysis of Pallidus MR1 data recorded across multi-subject and multi-day experiments.

The Pallidus MR1 system records timestamped activity measurements for multiple experimental subjects over extended periods generating rich behavioral time-series datasets that capture circadian rhythms, group dynamics and responses to pharmacological or genetic interventions. Analyzing these datasets manually using spreadsheets is slow, error-prone and difficult to scale across dozens of subjects or multiple experimental runs. Researchers need a way to rapidly explore temporal patterns, compare experimental groups and apply statistical validation without writing bespoke analysis scripts for each experiment.

I developed an interactive Python-based analysis platform that brings together time-series visualization, circadian segmentation, statistical hypothesis testing and automated Excel report generation into a single researcher-facing interface enabling fast, reproducible behavioral data analysis.

Problem Statement

Large-scale behavioral time-series datasets pose significant analytical challenges. Multi-subject experiments recorded over days or weeks produce high-dimensional data with complex temporal structure circadian cycles, treatment-response windows and inter-subject variability that is difficult to examine systematically in a spreadsheet.

Researchers also need to validate findings statistically, comparing treatment and control groups with appropriate tests. Without integrated tools, this typically requires custom code per experiment increasing time-to-insight and introducing reproducibility risks.

  • ↑ High dimensionality dozens of subjects recorded simultaneously
  • ↑ Long time horizons experiments spanning days or weeks
  • ↑ Circadian variability activity patterns shift across day/night
  • ↑ Group comparisons treatment vs control analysis required
Research Hypotheses

A core objective of the platform was to provide researchers with the statistical tools needed to validate specific behavioral hypotheses derived from neuroscience literature and experimental design. The platform was built around three primary hypotheses concerning circadian activity patterns and inter-subject dynamics.

H1
Nocturnal Activity Elevation
Subjects exhibit higher activity levels during the night compared to the day consistent with nocturnal behavioral patterns typically observed in rodent model systems.
t-test Day vs night activity comparison per subject and group
H2
Light/Dark Cycle Variability
Activity levels vary significantly between different light/dark cycles over the course of the experiment indicating a consistent circadian entrainment pattern rather than random fluctuation.
ANOVA Multi-cycle activity variance across the full recording period
H3
Inter-Subject Behavioral Influence
The activity of one subject influences the activity of others suggesting social or environmental coupling effects that may reflect neural circuit interactions or group behavioral dynamics.
ANOVA Cross-subject activity correlation and group-level comparison
Statistical methods used
  • t Independent t-test two-group comparisons (day vs night, treatment vs control)
  • F One-way ANOVA multi-group and multi-cycle comparisons
Capabilities
  • → Interactive time-series visualization with day/night highlights
  • → Custom group definition and side-by-side comparison
  • → SD bands and mean activity trend overlays
  • → Automated Excel report export with all statistics

Data Processing Pipeline
01
Dataset Upload & Ingestion
Researchers upload raw MR1 CSV datasets through the Streamlit interface. The system validates file structure, identifies device columns and prepares the dataset for processing.
Streamlit · File Upload
02
Data Cleaning & Standardization
Removes irrelevant metadata columns, handles missing timestamps and standardizes column naming conventions to ensure consistent downstream processing regardless of how the MR1 exports the data.
Pandas · Column Standardization
03
Timestamp Parsing & Resampling
Converts raw timestamp strings to datetime objects and resamples activity measurements to hourly intervals reducing noise and aligning data for temporal trend analysis and circadian comparisons.
Datetime Parsing · Hourly Resampling
04
Circadian Segmentation
Automatically segments records into day and night periods based on timestamp enabling separate analysis of diurnal and nocturnal activity patterns, which are critical for circadian behavioral research.
Day/Night Segmentation · Circadian Analysis
05
Group Aggregation & Statistics
Computes mean activity, standard deviation and group-level aggregation metrics for each experimental group. Applies t-tests and ANOVA to evaluate statistical significance of observed behavioral differences.
Pandas · SciPy · t-test · ANOVA
06
Visualization & Report Export
Renders interactive Plotly charts with day/night shading and SD bands and exports structured Excel reports via XlsxWriter containing all computed metrics, group comparisons and statistical test results.
Plotly · XlsxWriter · Excel Export
Metrics computed
  • μ Mean activity levels per subject and group
  • σ Standard deviation and variability bands
  • Δ Day vs night activity differentials
  • p p-values from t-test and ANOVA
  • Σ Group-level aggregation summaries

Challenges & Solutions
Challenge
Multi-Subject Scale
Experiments may contain dozens of subjects recorded simultaneously across multiple monitoring devices, making manual aggregation and comparison impractical at any meaningful scale.
Solution
Implemented efficient Pandas-based aggregation pipelines that process multi-device datasets and compute group-level statistics in a single pass, regardless of subject count.
Challenge
Circadian Temporal Structure
Behavioral activity varies significantly across day and night cycles a pattern that is invisible in raw data and easy to miss in standard time-series analysis without deliberate segmentation.
Solution
Implemented automatic day/night segmentation based on timestamps and hourly resampling, revealing circadian patterns clearly through shaded visualization and separate period statistics.
Challenge
Statistical Rigor
Neuroscience research requires rigorous statistical validation before conclusions can be drawn or results published often requiring different tests depending on group count and data distribution.
Solution
Integrated t-tests and ANOVA directly into the analysis platform via SciPy, enabling immediate hypothesis testing with p-value reporting without requiring any additional coding from researchers.
Outcome

The platform delivered a scalable, researcher-accessible behavioral analysis system for the KravitzLab supporting interactive exploration of behavioral datasets, automated day/night comparisons, integrated statistical hypothesis testing and streamlined report generation for research documentation.

By consolidating data cleaning, time-series visualization, circadian segmentation, group comparison and statistical testing into a single Streamlit application, the platform significantly improved research workflow efficiency reducing the time and technical overhead required to move from raw MR1 activity data to validated behavioral findings.
